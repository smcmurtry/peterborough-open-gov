Minutes of the AAC Built Environment Sub-Committee April 20, 2021 Accessibility Reviews a) Site Plan Approval for 159 Aylmer Street North, SPC-995


The committee made the following recommendations:

Page 2

1. Provide a larger landing at the intersection of the walkway to the sidewalk and

the walkway to the accessible parking space access aisle, to accommodate turning movements with a mobility aid.

2. Provide an automatic door operator for the main building entrance on the

verandah.

3. Avoid pavers on the accessible parking space and access aisle. The committee

has concern about product durability, even with the deep granular base specified, and recommends the requirement for a permeable surface be located on another area of the site. [Post meeting note: a member provided information on a permeable surface product called PurePave that is a singular surface vs individual pavers and recommended the City explore it as an option].

4. Provide a tactile attention indicator at the dropped curb for the accessible parking

space access aisle.

b) Zoning By-Law Amendment and Draft Plan of Subdivision Approval for 789

Lily Lake Road, Z2105SB and 15T-21501

The committee made the following recommendations:

1. Jeff requested the committee review a typical roundabout design and provide

comment for future roundabouts.

2. Add benches and garbage bins along the trail

3. Add shade covering at bench zones

4. Provide a parking solution for people who may come to the area to park and use

the trail, complete with accessible parking options.

5. Provide lighting on the trail.

6. Provide a detectable edge between the trail and the adjacent north/south street,

and provide accessible walkway connections from the street to the trail where appropriate.

7. Provide water fountains along the trail.

Minutes of the AAC Built Environment Sub-Committee April 20, 2021 c) Zoning By-Law Amendment for 2077 and 2011 Ashburnham Drive, Z2106


Page 3

Amanda Motyer left the meeting shortly after the presentation started.

The committee made the following recommendations:

1. The accessible parking location is compromised due to the location of the pick-up

/drop-off bay. Relocate accessible parking closer to the main entrance.

2. Relocate the walkway from the street to the site to have good pedestrian linkage

from the building entrance to the other side of the road.

3. Add an accessible pedestrian crossing to allow pedestrians to easily cross

Ashburnham Drive.

4. Provide a shelter and bench at the nearest transit stops.

5. Provide wayfinding signate to direct out of town visitors and people from

Peterborough to the site. Provide clear signage from the off-site parking lots to the Canoe Museum site, incorporating braille signage along pedestrian walkways. The Canoe Museum will host large events with people from out of town, such as the Wilderness Canoe Association conference which has an attendance of up to 500 people.

6. The reduced setback at the north end of the site means all access to the water is at the south end of the site. Due to the large site, provide a second access to the Trans Canada Trail from the north end of the site.

7. Provide a barrier free boat house and barrier-free access to the water. There are

many precedents of boat houses with water programs that are barrier free.

8. Increase the number of accessible parking spaces on the site instead of making

people with disabilities park in other existing off-site lots. The committee noted there are often other events that take place at the various amenities in the area (ie/ soccer tournaments) that result in fully occupied parking lots.

9. The committee requested the occupant load of the multipurpose room, exhibition

space and café be considered in the parking calculations.

10. The committee would like to further review the project at the Site Plan Control

stage. The committee would welcome a presentation from the Canadian Canoe Museum and their consultant, and provide feedback on how to make the building interior more accessible.
