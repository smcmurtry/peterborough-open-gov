Recreation Division Coordinator Report APRAC14-002 2014 Community Assistance Projects

Moved by Randy Turner

That the Arenas Parks and Recreation Advisory Committee approve the recommendations outlined in Report APRAC14-002 dated January 21, 2014, of the Recreation Division Coordinator, as follows:

a)

That partnering with Soul Beach Volleyball, through the Community Assistance Program, to establish two additional beach volleyball courts in Beavermead Park, in 2014, at an estimated cost to the City of $18,260 be endorsed;

That Staff be directed to:

Assist the residents of the Stewart Park neighbourhood to establish a neighbourhood association, with the goal of constructing a children’s play structure within Stewart Park, through the City’s Community Assistance Program; and

That Staff be directed to report back to the Arenas Parks and Recreation Advisory Committee once the association is established, the type of play structure is identified, and the estimated cost is determined.

Manager of the Arenas Division Report APRAC14-007 Presentation of the Arena Needs Assessment Project Draft Final Report

Moved by Dave Haacke

That the Arenas, Parks and Recreation Advisory Committee endorse the recommendations outlined in Report APRAC14-007, dated January 21, 2014, of the Manager of the Arenas Division, as follows:

a)

That the presentation of the Draft Report on the Arena Needs Assessment Study by Robert Lockhart, Rethink Group be received for information;

That the final Arena Needs Assessment Study be circulated to all neighbouring arena operators for review and that the Arenas Parks and Recreation Advisory Committee be encouraged to hold a forum with the regional operators to better understand the potential impact of the Needs Assessment;

That the Arena Needs Assessment Study be endorsed as a planning tool to inform and guide the feasibility study stage; and,

That advancing the project to the feasibility study stage for a new twin pad as a replacement facility for Northcrest Arena be endorsed; and that staff be directed to bring options back to APRAC for endorsement and to City Council for approval that address:
