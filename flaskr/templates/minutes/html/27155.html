Climate Change Initiatives Update

Report IPSIM20-003

Moved by Councillor Zippel

That Council approve the recommendations outlined in Report IPSIM20-003 dated March 2, 2020, of the Commissioner of Infrastructure and Planning Services, as follows:

That staff make application to the Federation of Canadian Municipalities- Green Municipal Fund; Study: Reduce Fossil Fuel Use in Fleet and that Council approve up to $100,000 from the Climate Change Reserve for the City’s 50% contribution, if approved;

That staff include anti-idling as a distinct initiative for inclusion in the City’s partnership program with Toronto and Region Conservation Authority on a neighbourhood based Low Carbon Social Mobilization initiative;

That, for projects/initiatives which have an impact on Climate Adaptation and/or Mitigation, a descriptive statement be included in future Standing Committee reports;

At the meeting of March 2, 2020 Committee amended d) as follows:

That staff report to Council annually on the progress related to the Climate Change Action Plan implementation plan, Adaptation and Community Resilience and Mitigation efforts including progress on the corporate target of 45 % Green House Gas emission reduction by 2030.

That staff report back on progress on the Peterborough Environmental Advisory Committee sub-committee’s review of striving for net zero emissions as a municipality, as part of annual update;

That staff prepare a report on the potential use of the remainder of the 2020 Contribution to the Climate Change Reserve in the amount of $426,400, by July 2020;

That Council endorse an 18-month contract position to develop Standard Operating procedures to optimize the use and potential of the adaptive controls for the City-wide streetlighting system and advance the planned Decorative Street lighting project;

That staff report to Council, as part of the 2021 budget review, on permanent staffing resources required to significantly advance and sustain the Corporate and Community actions related to the Climate Change Action Plan (CCAP);

That staff and Council seek opportunities to advocate with the Provincial and Federal governments for funding to support Climate Adaptation and Mitigation initiatives in Peterborough;

That staff and Council seek opportunities to advocate with the Provincial and Federal governments for policy changes which positively impact Climate Mitigation and Adaptation efforts and contribute to the benefit of the City and broader community;

That the City partner with Community agencies and researchers on initiatives which affect Climate Mitigation and Adaptation efforts for the broader community; and

That staff, in collaboration with key community partners, develop a clear implementation strategy for the Community Sector of the Climate Change Action Plan (CCAP), with focus on verifying data assumptions and identifying community measurables.
